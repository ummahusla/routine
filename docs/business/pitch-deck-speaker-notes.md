# Routine — Pitch Deck Speaker Notes

> 3-minute pitch. 7 slides. ~180s total budget. ~25–35s per content slide.

---

## Slide 1 — Intro (~10s)

- "Hi, I'm [name]. This is **Routine**."
- One-line hook: "Build any workflow automation by chatting with an agent — runs deterministically forever."

---

## Slide 2 — Problem (~30s)

- Workflow automation today is split between two broken halves.
- **Visual builders** (Zapier, Make, n8n) lock you into a catalog. Niche tool? Wait for the vendor or wire brittle HTTP blocks by hand.
- **AI agents** can talk to any API but re-explore every run. Non-deterministic, expensive, unauditable. Not production-safe.
- Hurts most: marketing agencies and ops consultancies. Every client has one weird tool nobody integrates with.

---

## Slide 3 — Solution (~35s)

- **Routine** = desktop builder + cloud runner. Chat-first.
- You describe the goal in plain English. The agent probes APIs, asks clarifying questions, builds the workflow live on the canvas.
- Once it works end-to-end, we **compile the trace into a deterministic script**. No LLM in the execution path. Runs in seconds, near-zero cost.
- LLM blocks only where judgment is needed — summarize, classify, generate copy.
- Tagline: "**Like Git for workflows** — version the intelligence, share it, reuse it."

---

## Slide 4 — Ideal Customer (~30s)

- **Primary:** digital marketing agencies, 10–100 people. Acute pain, real budgets, multiplier — one flow built, reused across N clients.
- **Secondary:** ops/automation consultancies billing clients for custom work.
- **Where we reach them:**
  - **Personal connections** — warm intros into 10 design-partner agencies.
  - **Product Hunt** launch for the OSS desktop app — high signal for ops/automation crowd.
  - **Hacker News** — Show HN with the OSS repo. Same playbook n8n used to hit 187K stars.

---

## Slide 5 — Market (~30s)

- Combined visible ARR: Zapier $310M, Make ~$75M, n8n $40M — **$400M+ and accelerating**.
- **Why now:** n8n grew 4× in 8 months after its AI pivot. Buyers want *agentic* automation, not visual builders. AI-native is the breakout move.
- OSS distribution is proven — n8n hit $2.5B valuation on free OSS + paid cloud.
- Beachhead: ~50K English-speaking agencies. 1% adoption at $5K ACV = $25M ARR ceiling on the beachhead alone. 10× from there with consultancies + RevOps.

---

## Slide 6 — Business Model (~25s)

- **n8n playbook**: free OSS desktop drives distribution, paid cloud runner monetizes.
- Tiers: Free OSS → Cloud Starter $19/seat → Team $99/seat → Business $249/seat → Enterprise self-hosted.
- **Structural margin advantage**: competitors pay LLM tokens forever on every run. Our flows are deterministic — COGS per run drops to near-zero after build. Higher margin ceiling than any agent-only player.
- Target: 75%+ gross margin, 4× LTV/CAC, 120%+ NRR.

---

## Slide 7 — Contacts (~10s)

- "Thank you. I'd love to talk to anyone running an agency or building automations."
- Email + LinkedIn + GitHub on screen. "Find me after."

---

## Pacing tips

- Practice with a timer. Cut anything that doesn't earn its seconds.
- Slides 3 and 5 are load-bearing — rehearse those twice.
- If running long, drop the tier table on slide 6 and just say "free OSS + paid cloud, n8n playbook, structural margin from deterministic execution."
