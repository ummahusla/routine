## Key Pricing Model Facts

From the pricing page FAQ:
> "Pricing based on monthly workflow executions, regardless of complexity."

> Executions defined as: single workflow runs regardless of step count

> Self-hosted instances: "combined usage from all instances where the license key is applied will count toward your quota"

> Overage pricing for Business: "4,000 EUR for extra buckets of 300,000 executions"

> Data storage: "data is stored within the EU — on servers located in Frankfurt, Germany"

From n8n's comparison post (https://blog.n8n.io/make-vs-zapier/):
> n8n charges "per workflow, no limit on tasks / steps" — versus Make (per operation) and Zapier (per task)

> n8n can be "1000 times more cost-efficient compared to Zapier or Make" for some automations

## Embedded / OEM Offering (n8n.embed)

Source: https://docs.n8n.io/embed/ and https://docs.n8n.io/hosting/oem-deployment/

n8n Embed is part of n8n's paid offering that allows you to white label n8n or incorporate it in your software as part of your commercial product.

**OEM Deployment Features:**
> n8n's OEM deployment option lets you embed and surface n8n's interface inside your own product's UI, allowing your users to build workflows, configure connections, and run workflow automation without leaving your product.

> n8n branding is required as part of an OEM integration.

Two models:
1. **OEM/Embed (interface visible):** Your users see n8n's workflow editor, branded within your product
2. **Backend only:** Your product calls n8n via webhook or API to trigger workflows; end users never see n8n

> OEM deployment is available on all paid plans under the standard license, with no separate agreement needed.

From the n8n second anniversary blog (https://blog.n8n.io/celebrating-n8n-second-anniversary/):
> "n8n.embed launched" (2021) — for product embedding

## Business Model Philosophy

From the seed round TechCrunch article (https://techcrunch.com/2020/03/13/n8n-a-fair-code-workflow-automation-platform-raises-seed-from-sequoia-as-vc-firm-steps-up-in-europe/):

Jan Oberhauser stated the model as: "Free and sustainable; open but pragmatic; community oriented; meritocratic and fair"

**Monetization Strategy (early 2020):**
- Free self-hosted version (permanently free)
- SaaS model launching with subscription fees
- Future consulting, support, and integration services
- Licensing fees for larger users

From the TechCrunch Series A article (https://techcrunch.com/2021/04/26/n8n-raises-12m-for-its-fair-code-approach-to-low-code-workflow-automation/):

> Self-hosted deployment is free with unlimited use. Cloud/SaaS usage requires payment. Revenue-sharing applies when embedding code in products.

From the Sacra research (https://sacra.com/c/n8n/):

> "execution-based pricing" where customers pay per complete workflow runs rather than per task.

> The company uses open-source as an acquisition funnel while monetizing through cloud, enterprise licenses, and embedded partnerships.

**Gross Margins:**
> "Exceeding 75%" (Sacra)

**Average Revenue Per Customer:**
> "~$13,300 annually" (Sacra)

## Cloud vs. Self-Hosted Strategic Positioning

From multiple sources, n8n's differentiation vs. Zapier/Make rests on:
1. Self-hosting capability (neither Zapier nor Make offer this)
2. Execution-based pricing (not per-step/per-task)
3. Unlimited users on all plans
4. Source code availability

From the pricing page:
> "No credit card required" for Starter/Pro free trials
> Business plan free trial requires credit card

From the Sacra analysis:
> Revenue split: ~55% cloud, ~30% enterprise licenses, ~15% embedded/OEM
