Source: Multiple — Taskade history article (https://www.taskade.com/blog/n8n-workflow-automation-history), Solo Founders article (https://solofounders.com/blog/how-a-solo-founder-turned-a-side-project-into-a-2-5b-workflow-automation-giant), Accel podcast (https://www.accel.com/podcast-episodes/bonus-n8ns-jan-oberhauser-on-building-the-excel-of-ai), Slush event profile (https://slush.org/program/jan-oberhauser), Turner Classic Movies database (https://www.tcm.com/tcmdb/person/12778176|0/Jan-Oberhauser/)

**Education:**
- Studied Audiovisual Media at Hochschule der Medien (Stuttgart Media University), 2005–2009

**VFX Career:**
- Worked at Academy Award-winning VFX studios on major productions
- Films included: Disney's *Maleficent* and *Happy Feet Two*
- Studios: Digital Domain, Pixomondo, and Neon
- Role evolved from doing effects to becoming a "pipeline TD" — automating workflows for VFX artists

**From the Accel podcast:**
> Jan "did effects from movies originally" and later became a "pipeline entity" focused on making "the life of the artist more efficient, easier, which obviously involved a lot of automation."

**On the VFX automation insight** (from search results):
> "Those people — very smart, very well paid, and quite technical — they were always reliant on me or other people like myself to actually do the things they wanted done. They could have had so much more impact if they would have been empowered. And they weren't."

**Prior ventures:**
- showreel.tv — started while still in college

## The Building of n8n (2018–2019)

Source: n8n second anniversary blog (https://blog.n8n.io/celebrating-n8n-second-anniversary/), Accel podcast, Solo Founders article

**Why he built it — the core problem:**
> "I spent probably 90% of my time re-implementing things that have been implemented before. The most basic example is something like Get us down GitHub, send a message to Slack." — Jan Oberhauser, Accel podcast

From the n8n second anniversary blog:
> Jan created n8n because automating small tasks was time-consuming, requiring documentation reading, coding, GitHub commits, server deployment, error handling, SSL setup, and crash recovery.

**Timeline of early development:**
- **2018:** First implementation began (side project while working at link.fish)
- **~1.5 years:** Time between initial idea and public launch, while working other jobs to support his family
- **June 23, 2019:** First version published to GitHub
- **October 2019:** Product Hunt and Hacker News launch ("proper launch")

From the Accel podcast:
> "1.5 years between initial idea and launch while working other startup jobs to support family"

**Declining Y Combinator:**
From TechCrunch seed round article (https://techcrunch.com/2020/03/13/n8n-a-fair-code-workflow-automation-platform-raises-seed-from-sequoia-as-vc-firm-steps-up-in-europe/):
> Oberhauser **declined Y Combinator to remain in Berlin with family.**

## June 23, 2019 — First GitHub Commit and Soft Launch

Source: n8n second anniversary blog (https://blog.n8n.io/celebrating-n8n-second-anniversary/), Taskade history article

**What happened:**
> The first version was published on GitHub on June 23, 2019. Jan shared it via alternativeto.net and Quora — not on major tech channels yet.

From the Solo Founders article:
> "June 23, 2019: First n8n version published"

**Early organic discovery:**
> Soon after making n8n public on GitHub, people started to report issues, open PRs, and send emails — "people around the world were using the tool and getting value out of it — investing their time to contribute to the codebase."

## October 2019 — Product Hunt and Hacker News Launch

Source: Taskade history article (https://www.taskade.com/blog/n8n-workflow-automation-history), n8n second anniversary blog, DEV community article (https://dev.to/heetvekariya/what-is-n8n-nodemation-n8n-o7a)

**The launch:**
> "In October 2019, Oberhauser felt that n8n was ready to be presented to a wider audience, so he launched it on ProductHunt and HackerNews."

**Initial response on Product Hunt:**
> Developers loved the concept — a self-hostable automation tool with a visual interface and growing integration library. The launch generated significant buzz.

**Initial response on Hacker News:**
> When n8n hit Hacker News, eagle-eyed open-source advocates noticed that n8n's license wasn't actually open source in the traditional sense (Apache 2.0 with Commons Clause).

**The licensing controversy:**
> "There's no better marketing for a developer tool than a passionate Hacker News debate about licensing."

From Jan's blog post (https://blog.n8n.io/fair-code-for-sustainable-open-source-alternatives/):
> A GitHub issue titled "It's gaslighting to call it open source" accumulated over **330 comments**.
> Hacker News changed the post title from something like "n8n.io – Open source Zapier alternative" to "n8n.io – Workflow automation alternative to Zapier."

## First Community Contributor: Ricardo Espinoza

Source: n8n blog (https://blog.n8n.io/code-contributor-to-integration-developer/), HackerNoon article (https://hackernoon.com/inspiring-story-of-a-victorious-n8n-expert-that-will-give-you-motivation-jwk3t5f)

**Background:**
> Ricardo Espinoza is a software engineer originally from Venezuela who earned his degree in Informatics Engineering in 2014. He relocated to the United States that same year.

**Discovery of n8n:**
> Ricardo discovered n8n in **October 2019** through **Product Hunt**. He describes his initial reaction as "love at first sight" after viewing the explainer video.

**His motivation:**
> His motivation stemmed from needing automation tools for weekly manual tasks at his job — specifically reading emails from Google Sheets and sending transactional emails via Mandrill.

**First contribution:**
> He created his first contribution by building a **Mandrill node**, which took several weeks to complete.

**Scale of contributions:**
> Ricardo went on to develop over **60 nodes** for the platform. He credits Jan (CEO and founder) with being instrumental to his success, noting that Jan "always answered all the questions" and "provided guidance throughout the process."

**Jan's personal engagement:**
> Jan "personally thanked him for each contribution"

**Employment:**
> In **March 2020**, Ricardo joined n8n full-time as an **integration developer**. He focuses primarily on nodes and community support.

**Why n8n over competitors:**
> He chose n8n over competitors like Zapier and Parabola because of its "open-source code, Docker deployment options, existing integrations, user experience, extensibility, and welcoming community."

**Advice:**
> "Find a project that you are excited about and is aligned with your skillset. That excitement is what would keep you going."

**From the Accel podcast:**
> "Ricardo, an early contributor from Florida discovered n8n on Product Hunt and created 50-60 integrations before being hired as an employee."

## Sequoia Seed Round and First Paid Customers (March–April 2020)

Source: TechCrunch (https://techcrunch.com/2020/03/13/n8n-a-fair-code-workflow-automation-platform-raises-seed-from-sequoia-as-vc-firm-steps-up-in-europe/)

**What caught Sequoia's attention:**
From the Solo Founders article:
> "From the start, Jan treated community as foundational — the company's first hire was a **developer evangelist**, which caught Sequoia's attention and led to n8n's $1.5 million seed round in March 2020."

**Timeline around seed:**
- March 2020: $1.5M seed funding from Sequoia + firstminute
- April 2020: Jan transitioned to full-time on n8n, hired first team members
- April 2020 onwards: First commercial elements introduced (SaaS subscription model launching)

From TechCrunch (seed round article):
> Jan stated the SaaS model was "launching the following month" (April 2020) with subscription fees
> Future plans: "consulting, support, and integration services (expected launch in one year)"
> "Capability to be deployed to 1 million people while remaining free"

**Community at seed time:**
> 10,000+ GitHub stars by late 2019, growing through seed round

## First Enterprise Customers and Early Growth (2021)

Source: TechCrunch Series A (https://techcrunch.com/2021/04/26/n8n-raises-12m-for-its-fair-code-approach-to-low-code-workflow-automation/)

- Team: 16 people
- GitHub stars: 13,000+

- April 2021: $12M Series A funding

**Future goals stated at anniversary:**
- Release n8n version 1.0
- Implement user management functionality
- Create certification courses
- Continuous node creation and UI redesign

> "I've always been a supporter of open-source projects and believe that source code should be available to everyone. That is why I chose to make n8n openly available and fair-code."

## Founder's Own Description of the Early Pain Point

From TechCrunch seed round article:
> Built as a side project while Oberhauser worked part-time elsewhere

From Accel podcast:
> "We have a steep learning curve" but users gain significant value once proficient.

From Jan's blog (fair-code post):
> "I knew that if I open-source n8n, someone would come along and offer a hosted service on top of it."
> "I'm not building n8n for free because I'm a good person. I genuinely need to build a sustainable company"
