## Co-Founder: Wade Foster — CEO

Source: Mizzou Engineering (https://engineering.missouri.edu/2022/150-years-of-leadership-wade-foster/); Clay.com (https://www.clay.com/dossier/zapier-ceo); Wikipedia (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zapier)

**Full name:** Wade Foster
**Title:** Co-Founder and CEO
**Founded Zapier:** October 1, 2011 (from Clay.com)
**Age at founding:** 24

**Education:**
- B.S. Industrial Engineering, University of Missouri-Columbia, 2005–2009
- MBA, University of Missouri-Columbia, 2009–2010

From the Mizzou Engineering feature:
> "In 2011, at the age of 24, Foster and Bryan Helming (BS Finance '11) began working on an idea"

**Pre-Zapier career:**
- Student engineer, Missouri Industrial Assessment Center
- Member of jazz band at Mizzou (where he met Bryan Helmig)
- Marketing intern at software company in Columbia
- Freelance web developer (websites, email marketing templates, web apps)
- Email Marketing Manager, Veterans United Home Loans, May–December 2011

**Departure from Veterans United:**
> Foster left Veterans United in December 2011 to work on Zapier full-time.

## Co-Founder: Bryan Helmig — CTO

Source: Changelog Founders Talk #55 (https://changelog.com/founderstalk/55); Clay.com (https://www.clay.com/dossier/zapier-cto); LinkedIn

**Full name:** Bryan Helmig
**Title:** Co-Founder and CTO
**Description:** "CTO/Co-founder at Zapier, jazz/blues musician"

**Education:** BS Finance, University of Missouri (class of 2011)

**Pre-Zapier:**

- Self-taught developer
- Employee at Veterans United Home Loans (where he met Foster; they would "chat over IM about new ideas")
- Connected with Mike Knoop through Hacker News community in Columbia

**Departure from Veterans United:**
> Helmig left Veterans United in April 2012 to join Zapier full-time.

**Bryan Helmig Quotes:**

From Changelog Founders Talk #55 (https://changelog.com/founderstalk/55):

> "We were all also working at this company in Columbia called Veterans United… we were always chatting over the IM about like 'Maybe it'll this idea, or that idea.'"

> "The one we ended up pitching was called – I think we called it API Mixer at the time."

> "We hacked all weekend, and I don't think we slept more than maybe six hours amongst all of us over the 54 hours."

> "When you're a remote company, especially remote-first, and for us remote-only, is you have to start to put in place structures for communication earlier."

## Remote-First / Fully Distributed Culture

Source: Wikipedia; zapier.com/jobs (fetched May 2026); multiple interview sources

### Origin of Remote Culture

From Wikipedia (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zapier):
> Zapier "has operated fully remotely since its founding"

The founders started in Columbia, Missouri, relocated to the San Francisco Bay Area for Y Combinator, but maintained distributed hiring from the beginning — never requiring employees to be in the same location.

### Core Cultural Values (from zapier.com/jobs, fetched May 2026)

1. **AI-First Mindset**: Employees should "believe in automation and AI's accessibility and embrace experimentation"
2. **Innovation**: Teams "continuously improve processes and tackle complex challenges collaboratively"
3. **Customer Focus**: "Decisions prioritize customer experience and business growth"
4. **Diversity & Inclusion**: Organization "commits to supporting underrepresented groups through structured programs"
5. **Transparency**: Company practices "default to transparency" in communications
6. **Feedback Culture**: Employees "encouraged to embrace feedback and support colleague growth"

### Remote Work Benefits (from zapier.com/jobs)

> Performance-based pay with bonuses and equity
> "Live Well Budget" for health/wellness resources
> "Work Well Budget" for remote setup support
> Fertility and wellness coaching programs
> Learning budgets for conferences and skill development
> Coaching and 360° feedback programs
> "Next Play Program" supporting internal and external career transitions

### Remote Recognition

> In 2024, Zapier earned recognition as a **"Top 100 Most Flexible Employer globally"** (from zapier.com/jobs)

### Wade Foster on Remote Advantages

From HackerNoon interview:
> "Retaining people is easier when they're able to work from home. People get to trade in commute time for extra time."

From the Taskade history article:
> "Our retention rates are so much better because working remotely aligns personal and work lives, creating continuity and knowledge hard to build with constant turnover."

From the 20VC podcast:
> "Working remotely is not so different than working in an office... people want to feel like they have autonomy, mastery, purpose."

### The De-Location Package (March 2017)

From Inc. Magazine (https://www.inc.com/sonya-mann/zapier-remote-work-de-location.html):
> "$10,000 de-location package for new employees" wanting to leave the Bay Area
> Reimbursement of up to $10,000 of moving costs incurred within the first three months
> Employees asked to commit to Zapier for one year
> Described as "the first [de-location package] of its kind"
> By October 2017: "no Zapier employees had actually chosen to take the money and relocate, although job applicants had increased 53% since the package's rollout"

### AirBnBOnboarding Practice

From the YC blog (https://www.ycombinator.com/blog/the-ceo-who-pays-employees-to-de-locate-from-the-bay):

Wade Foster:
> "we do this thing called AirBnBOnboarding, which when we hire folks within the first month, we actually do like to have them spend a week in person"
