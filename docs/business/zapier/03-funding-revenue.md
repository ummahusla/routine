> Raw research dossier. All quotes are verbatim from primary sources. Every fact includes a source URL.

## Total Capital Raised

Zapier has raised approximately **$2.68 million** across seed rounds only — an exceptionally small amount for a company generating $310M+ in annual revenue.

From the Taskade history article (https://www.taskade.com/blog/zapier-history):

> "Total Capital Raised: $2.68 million across seed rounds only"
> - 2012: $1.3M seed (Y Combinator batch + Bessemer/DFJ)
> - 2013–2014: ~$1.38M additional seed funding

From the Contrary Research report (https://research.contrary.com/company/zapier):

> "Total Venture Funding: $1.4 million seed (October 2012)"
> "ARR to Funding Ratio: 100x (raised $1.4M, achieved $140M ARR)"

Note on discrepancy: Different sources cite $1.4M vs. $2.68M. The $2.68M figure likely reflects total across two seed rounds (Oct 2012 + Nov 2014). The $1.4M figure reflects the single most-cited round. Zapier's founders have consistently described the company as raising "less than $2 million" in total equity.

From the Mosaic analysis (https://www.mosaic.tech/post/moves-that-got-zapier-to-140m-arr):

> "Taking $1.4M in venture capital and turning it into a $140M ARR business is a remarkable feat of capital efficiency."

> "Zapier achieved a 100x ratio of ARR to funding, placing it alongside bootstrapped successes like Atlassian (128x) and Cloudinary (80x)."

## Funding Round 1: Seed — October 2012

**Amount:** $1.2 million
**Date:** October 31, 2012
**Lead investors:** Bessemer Venture Partners (BVP), Draper Fisher Jurvetson (DFJ)
**Other investors:** Wufoo co-founder Kevin Hale, Brant Bukowsky, Brent Beshore, Michael Liou, Kim Miller, Jon Murray, Mike Stachowiak, Ben Li

Source: TechCrunch (https://techcrunch.com/2012/10/31/zapier-raises-1-2m-seed-round/)

From TechCrunch:

> "The Zapier platform builds on the movement of enterprise software to the cloud, and is already seeing great traction with its third-party developer products achieving tremendous growth in a very short period of time." — DFJ spokesperson

**Company metrics at time of this round:**
- Added over 80 web applications since launch
- Supported over 116 services
- Developer platform launched August 2012
- Company launched June 2012
- Pricing at launch: "Free limited version available; premium accounts started at $15/month"

**Round timeline:** "Closed in about two and a half weeks around the time of Y Combinator demo day" — Silicon Prairie News (https://siliconprairienews.com/2012/10/zapier-raises-1-2-million-seed-round-hires-first-employee/)

**Use of funds:** Product iteration, customer support system improvements, hiring one to two full-time developers.

**First employee hired with this capital:** Micah Bennett (Chicago) — hired specifically to improve customer support.

**Kevin Hale connection:** Kevin Hale co-founded Wufoo (one of Zapier's most important early integrations), then went on to become a Y Combinator partner (2013–2020). His investment in Zapier reflected the strategic overlap between form-capture (Wufoo's domain) and data routing (Zapier's domain).

## Funding Round 2: Seed — November 2014

**Amount:** $1.36 million
**Date:** November 25, 2014
**Lead investor:** Sequoia Capital

Source: Tracxn (https://tracxn.com/d/companies/zapier/)

This was Sequoia's first investment in Zapier before the later secondary transaction in 2021. The round was a small follow-on seed, not a Series A.

## Y Combinator Program — Summer 2012 (S12)

From the YC company profile (https://ycombinator.com/companies/zapier):

> Batch: Summer 2012 (S12)
> "Initial Funding: $1.3 million in seed funding from Y Combinator and Bessemer Venture Partners"

**Zapier was rejected on first application (Winter 2012 batch)**:

From the Taskade history article:
> "The founders applied to Y Combinator, a Silicon Valley startup incubator, but were initially rejected. They reapplied the following year and were accepted."

From the Changelog podcast with Bryan Helmig (https://changelog.com/founderstalk/55):
> "The first time they just flat out rejected us. It was a nice email… Also, a hot tip for folks out there - if you want a good rejection email, go find the Y Combinator one."

**Second application success:**

By the time of the S12 application, the founders had: "a working functional beta that a thousand people had paid to get access to, plus 10,000 people on a launch list"

From the Zapier blog about their YC journey (https://zapier.com/blog/3-dudes-missouri-built-product-found-paying-customers-and-got-yc/):

> The founders wore matching orange Zapier t-shirts to the interview.
> Paul Graham told them to "stop trying to get to the demo and just answer the questions"
> After a wait on a patio: "Hey Bryan, its Paul. We loved you guys, we'd like to fund you."

From Bryan Helmig on the YC summer:
> "That summer we just worked; I don't think we slept a whole lot, we'd just work-work-work. We worked 16-hour days pretty regularly."

> "The magic trick was we weren't doing that to get into Y Combinator, like, at all… That's just how you build a business."

## Secondary Market Transaction — January 2021

**Type:** Secondary share purchase (not a primary funding round — no new capital to company)
**Date:** January 2021
**Buyers:** Sequoia Capital and Steadfast Capital Management
**Sellers:** Early investors (NOT founders)
**Valuation established:** $5 billion
**Founder participation:** None — all three co-founders declined to sell shares

Source: Wikipedia (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zapier); Startupbooted (https://www.startupbooted.com/zapier-valuation-secrets-the-hidden-growth-story-that-shocked-silicon-valley); The Information (reported via https://regtechafrica.com/sequoia-buys-shares-in-elusive-startup-zapier-at-multibillion-dollar-valuation/)

From Wikipedia:

> "In January 2021, investment firms Sequoia Capital and Steadfast Financial purchased shares from some of Zapier's early investors in a secondary market transaction. The transaction valued the company at US$5 billion."

**The Information headline:**
> "Sequoia Buys Shares in Elusive Startup Zapier at Multibillion-Dollar Valuation"

**ARR at time of secondary:** $140 million (trailing ARR when the transaction occurred)
**Revenue multiple at $5B valuation:** 30.3x
**Founder equity retention:** ~80% owned by founding team as of 2021

From Forbes interview with Wade Foster (reported via startupbooted.com):

> "For us, we've always looked at financing events, whether they're primary, secondary or public markets, as a tool in the tool belt. It's something that you can reach for as a person who runs a business that can help you when you need it... I think that's a much healthier approach to things than sort of getting on a hamster wheel that is difficult to get off."

From Wade Foster separately:

> "If I were to take money out, the first thing I would do is find more Zapier shares"

From the 20VC podcast (https://www.deciphr.ai/podcast/20vc-scaling-zapier-to-140m-arr-and-a-5bn-valuation-on-14m-of-funding-what-founders-misunderstand-about-fundraising--how-founders-should-think-about-secondaries-today-with-wade-foster-founder--ceo--zapier):

> "Taking a little bit of money off the table in a secondary is, I generally think, the risk reward equation there is probably pretty reasonably smart."

> "You are not raising money. You are selling a part of your company."

## Acquisitions (Strategic M&A)

From Wikipedia (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zapier):

**1. Makerpad — March 2021** (Zapier's first acquisition)
- Target: Makerpad — no-code education service and community
- Terms: Undisclosed
- Makerpad metrics at acquisition: 8,000+ members, ~$400,000 ARR, user base expanded 4x in under one year
- Ben Tossell (founder): "stand-alone entity structure following acquisition"
- Tossell on deal: "a 'no-brainer' compared to alternative routes such as VC funding"
- How it happened: Wade Foster saw a tweet shared in Slack and reached out

From TechCrunch (https://techcrunch.com/2021/03/08/zapier-buys-no-code-focused-makerpad-in-its-first-acquisition/):
> Zapier at time of acquisition: 400 employees, "crossed $100 million ARR mark last summer [2020]"

**2. Vowel — March 2024**
- Target: Vowel — AI-powered video conferencing tool
- Terms: Undisclosed
- Vowel services were sunsetted December 27, 2023 before acquisition
- Announced alongside launch of Zapier Central
- Vowel CEO Andrew Berman became Director of AI at Zapier

From the press release (https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/zapier-leads-the-evolution-of-ai-automation-with-acquisition-of-vowel-and-launch-of-zapier-central-302081740.html):

> Sheryl Soo, SVP New Products: "The acquisition of Vowel enhances our AI capabilities and furthers our mission of democratizing AI and automation."

> Andrew Berman, Director of AI: "We were customers of Zapier, using them as our integration layer, and envisioned Vowel evolving into an AI command center."

**3. NoCodeOps — July 2024**
- Target: NoCodeOps — Atlanta-based no-code platform
- Terms: Undisclosed
- NoCodeOps had raised approximately $5.5 million from Hustle Fund and Charge Ventures

**4. Utopian Labs — October 2025**
- Target: Utopian Labs (formerly Luna.ai) — AI sales email writing tool
- Terms: Undisclosed
- Founded 2024, based in Palo Alto
- Operations of Utopian Labs ceased the following month (per Wikipedia)
