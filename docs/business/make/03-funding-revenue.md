## Part 1: Integromat — Pre-Acquisition History (Bootstrapped, Czech Origins)

### Founding and Origins

Integromat was launched in 2012 in Prague, Czech Republic. The project originated as an internal tool — a way to save the founders time — before the team realized it could become a cloud integration platform for others.

The founding team included six people:
- **Ondřej Gazda** — CEO
- **Patrik Šimek** — CTO
- **Michal Toman** — Platform Engineer
- **Pavel Duras** — CFO
- **Tomáš Schel** — Investor
- **Roman Bršlica** — Investor

Source: Tracxn company profile (https://tracxn.com/d/companies/integromat/__0uq9kdCRGqnh8fjK828EpkH10XIp4ZUTS6DU1gcryu0)

Note on founding date: Some sources cite 2012 as the launch year for the product, while others cite 2016 as the formal incorporation of the startup entity. The most likely explanation is that the product was built as an internal project beginning around 2012, with the company formally organized around 2016 when it began serving external customers at scale.

### Bootstrapped — Zero External Funding Raised

Integromat raised no venture capital or external investment at any point prior to its acquisition by Celonis. The company grew entirely on its own revenue.

From CzechStartups.gov.cz interview with Ondřej Gazda and Patrik Šimek (https://czechstartups.gov.cz/en/novinky/interview-with-ondrej-gazda-and-patrik-simek-on-an-investment-that-resonates-with-the-startup-world-the-czech-company-integromat-was-acquired-by-the-german-firm-celonis-for-a-price-of-more-than-czk-2/):

> "The company was financed from their own resources without investor help in the beginning, and during the span of a few years they managed to grow on the market without investor assistance before being sold for more than 100 million dollars."

From GetLatka (https://getlatka.com/companies/integromat):

> "Integromat has bootstrapped to over 250K customers. Integromat was launched in 2012 and has not raised any money."

The founders declined investment offers — including reportedly turning down Celonis's acquisition approach three times before eventually accepting. From the same CzechStartups interview:

> "The co-founders' philosophy was different from typical startups — one of the biggest roles in Integromat's success was played by the fact that they did not build it up in order to sell it later."

A Czech-language article in CzechCrunch (https://cc.cz/odmitli-20-milionu-obratem-jim-nabidli-200-cesky-startup-nakonec-prodali-za-miliardy/) with the headline "Odmítli 20 milionů, obratem jim nabídli 200" ("They rejected 20 million, immediately were offered 200") describes how Integromat turned down funding offers before eventually accepting the Celonis acquisition. The co-founders turned down investment even "when they approached nine-figure dollar amounts, though it was sometimes really difficult."

## Part 3: Celonis Funding History

### Company Background

Celonis SE was founded on June 8, 2011, by **Alexander (Alex) Rinke**, **Bastian Nominacher**, and **Martin Klenk** as a spin-off from the Technical University of Munich (TUM). The founders had studied mathematics, computer science, and finance and information management, and built a prototype that automatically reconstructed business processes from event logs during a student consultancy project for Bayerischer Rundfunk.

The company ran bootstrapped for its first five years, funded by TUM's EXIST founder scholarship, with large corporations like Siemens trialing the product as early as 2011. No external venture funding was taken for the first five years.

Sources: Wikipedia (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celonis); TUM Community (https://www.community.tum.de/en/celonis/)

## Part 7: Layoffs and Restructuring at Celonis

### February 2023 Layoffs

Celonis laid off approximately **120 employees** in February 2023.

From Blind discussions and reporting (https://www.teamblind.com/post/celonis-layoffs-xjer4jut):

> "In February 2023, Celonis laid off 120 employees. The company laid off people due to overhiring and leadership projecting unattainable growth metrics."

## Summary: Make's Capital Efficiency Profile

Make (Integromat) represents one of Europe's most notable bootstrapped exits:

- Built from 2012 to 2020 with **zero external venture capital**
- Sold for **>$100M** (>CZK 2.5B) — entirely bootstrapped
- Grew from ~$10M revenue at acquisition to **$52.6M+** revenue by 2025
- Grew from 375,000 users at acquisition to **3.1 million** users by end of 2024
- Grew from 63 employees in 2020 to **826** (peak, December 2023); consolidated to ~478–680 by 2025–2026
- Operates as a profitable business unit within a $13B parent company that has itself raised $1.77B in venture capital

The Integromat / Make story is a parallel to Zapier's capital efficiency narrative but with a different endpoint: where Zapier stayed independent, Integromat chose acquisition — achieving a $100M+ exit as a fully bootstrapped Czech startup.
