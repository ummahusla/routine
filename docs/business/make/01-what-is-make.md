## The Scenarios Model — Core Architecture

### Scenarios

From the Make Help Center — Scenarios page (https://help.make.com/scenarios):

> "A scenario is a series of modules that indicate how data should be transferred and transformed between apps/services in Make."

> "A scenario is an automated workflow."

> "In Make, you can build an automation using a parent scenario and one or more subscenarios, where a parent scenario is the main scenario that triggers one or more subscenarios via the Scenarios > Call a Scenario module."

### Modules — Types

From the Make Help Center — Types of Modules (https://help.make.com/types-of-modules):

> "There are four types of modules in Make based on what they do with data: triggers, searches, actions, and universal modules."

### Triggers

From the Make Help Center — Scenarios documentation (https://help.make.com/scenarios):

> "A trigger tracks changes in a service and brings them to Make so you can use the data in your scenario."

> "A trigger only shows new data from your service account each time a scenario processes."

> "You can add a trigger only once in the scenario as the first module."

### Operations

From the Make Help Center — Operations page (https://help.make.com/operations):

> "An operation is a single module run to process data or check for new data. When you run a scenario, each of its modules runs one or more times, resulting in 1 or more operations."

### Routers

From the Make Help Center — Router page (https://help.make.com/router):

> "A router allows you to branch the flow into several chains of modules. Each route processes the data differently according to the condition you set."

### Iterators

From the Make Help Center — Aggregator page and How-To guide (https://help.make.com/aggregator, https://www.make.com/en/how-to-guides/how-to-use-iterator-array-aggregator-in-make):

> "An iterator is a special type of module that allows you to split a larger bundle into multiple smaller bundles. For that purpose, iterators work only with arrays."

### Aggregators

From the Make Help Center — Aggregator page (https://help.make.com/aggregator):

> "An aggregator is a type of module designed to merge several bundles of data into a single bundle. When you run an aggregator, it accumulates all the bundles it receives (during a single source module's operation) and outputs a single bundle with an array containing one item per each accumulated bundle."

### Webhooks

From the Make Help Center — Webhooks page (https://help.make.com/webhooks):

> "Webhooks allow you to send data to Make over HTTP. Webhooks create a URL that you can call from an external app or service, or from another Make scenario. Use webhooks to trigger the execution of scenarios."

### Data Stores

From the Make Help Center — Data Stores page (https://help.make.com/data-stores):

> "Use data stores as databases to store and manage data across scenarios."

Sources: https://help.make.com/scenarios, https://help.make.com/types-of-modules, https://help.make.com/router, https://help.make.com/aggregator, https://help.make.com/webhooks, https://help.make.com/data-stores, https://help.make.com/operations

## License Model

Make.com is a **proprietary, closed-source, cloud-only SaaS platform**. There is no self-hosted option.

From the Make vs n8n official comparison page (https://www.make.com/en/compare/make-vs-n8n):

> "Make.com cannot be self-hosted and is exclusively a cloud SaaS product."

> "Make provides 3,000+ company-maintained integrations for trustworthy, streamlined setup of popular and niche apps, plus the ability to use custom APIs and code on paid plans."

> "Cloud-based Make offers better reliability, tighter security, and lower total cost of ownership for most use cases."

From the Make Security page (https://www.make.com/en/security):

- **SOC 2 Type II** certified
- **SOC 3** certified
- **ISO 27001** certified
- **GDPR** compliant
- Data encryption in transit and at rest
- Single Sign-On (SSO) available on Enterprise

**Enterprise deployment:** Make's Enterprise platform runs in a separately managed AWS environment, isolated from self-service cloud customers.

Sources: https://www.make.com/en/compare/make-vs-n8n, https://www.make.com/en/security, https://www.make.com/en/enterprise

## Comparisons

### Make vs Zapier

From Make's official blog post (https://www.make.com/en/blog/make-vs-zapier, published January 8, 2025):

**On app integrations:**
> "Make supports 3,000+ app integrations, of which 560 are AI apps, and generally provides roughly double the number of preset actions per app compared to Zapier."

> "While Zapier lets you integrate 8,000+ apps and is possibly the most-connected AI automation platform out there."

> "Make generally offers roughly double the number of preset actions per app, meaning you can automate more within each integration without custom workarounds."

**On visual design:**
> "Make takes a visual-first approach to workflow automation."

> "In contrast, Zapier's automations editor is more linear, rigid, and table-like. You can move your Zaps' branches around, but less flexibly. As a result, visualizing your automation's data paths and getting buy-in from business stakeholders can be more challenging."

**On Make Grid vs Zapier Canvas:**
> "Make Grid is a real-time visual orchestration map included in all Make paid plans. It automatically visualizes the relationships between your scenarios, AI agents, sub-scenarios, and data flows across your entire automation landscape – without any manual setup. Unlike Zapier Canvas, Make Grid reflects your live setup at all times."

**On support and community:**
> "Make provides professional support on all paid plans, with 24/7 priority customer support for Enterprise customers."

> "Additional resources available to all users at no extra cost include: Make Community forum with 45,000+ registered members · Make Academy with 38 interactive courses across five learning paths, covering foundational concepts to advanced agentic automation · Help Center with documentation, setup walkthroughs, and best practices."

**On pricing:**
> "Make is cheaper and lets you automate more types of app actions."

Source: https://www.make.com/en/blog/make-vs-zapier

### Make vs n8n

From Make's official comparison page (https://www.make.com/en/compare/make-vs-n8n, as of 2026):

**On integrations:**
> "Make provides 3,000+ company-maintained integrations for trustworthy, streamlined setup of popular and niche apps, plus the ability to use custom APIs and code on paid plans."

> "n8n offers 1,200+ pre-built integrations plus, if self-hosted, the option to develop and use community nodes that carry security risks."

**On deployment:**
> "Make.com cannot be self-hosted and is exclusively a cloud SaaS product."

> "Cloud-based Make offers better reliability, tighter security, and lower total cost of ownership for most use cases."

> "Meanwhile, self-hosted n8n provides superior customization that only a fraction of highly resourced organizations truly need."

**On pricing:**
> "Make employs a credit-based pricing model; as of November 2025, operations have fully transitioned to credits as the billing unit."

> "n8n relies on a pricier per-execution model, while its self-hosted options often hide significant infrastructure and maintenance costs."

**On ease of use:**
> "Make foregrounds visual low- and no-code principles to enable users just starting out, and the Make platform's drag-and-drop interface allows users to build automated workflows within the visual Scenario Builder."

> "While n8n offers deep developer customization, it requires significantly higher technical overhead and maintenance compared to Make's intuitive, enterprise-grade speed."

## Billing: Switch from Operations to Credits (August 27, 2025)

From the Make Help Center — "Introducing credits: new billing unit live in Make" (https://help.make.com/introducing-credits-new-billing-unit-live-in-make):

> "Starting August 27, 2025, billing shifts to credits, converting operations 1:1, while maintaining plans and enabling dynamic usage tracking for advanced features."

From the Make Community announcement post (https://community.make.com/t/introducing-credits-a-new-system-of-billing/89480):

> "Effective August 27th, 2025, we're replacing operations with credits as our billing unit, allowing for more flexible pricing that better reflects your actual usage of Make."

> "This update allows them to introduce different credit values for different actions, meaning billing will better reflect the specific actions performed within the platform."

From the Make Help Center — Credits page (https://help.make.com/credits):

> "Credits have replaced operations as the term for Make's billing unit, and your existing plan and pricing, including the cost of buying credits, remain unchanged."

> "For non-AI apps, 1 operation equals 1 credit."

> "For third party AI apps like OpenAI, Anthropic Claude, and Gemini, 1 operation equals 1 credit, though credits for modules with automatic AI provider connections also include token usage."

> "Existing operations on August 27th automatically converted to credits at a 1:1 ratio."

From the Make Help Center — Credits and Operations page (https://help.make.com/credits-and-operations):

> "Operations now refer to individual module runs that process or check for data, and credits are what you buy and consume for those operations and other usage-based factors."

### Pricing Plans (Post-August 2025, Credit-Based)

- **Free plan:** 1,000 credits per month, up to 2 active scenarios, 15-minute minimum interval between scheduled scenario runs
- **Core plan:** From ~€9/month; 10,000+ credits; execution intervals reduced to 1 minute
- **Pro plan:** Higher credit allotments; advanced features
- **Teams plan:** ~$29/month (billed annually); 10,000 credits per month (scalable); team collaboration, shared scenario templates, team role management
- **Enterprise:** Custom pricing; 24/7 priority customer support; dedicated AWS environment

Sources: https://help.make.com/introducing-credits-new-billing-unit-live-in-make, https://help.make.com/credits, https://community.make.com/t/introducing-credits-a-new-system-of-billing/89480, https://help.make.com/credits-and-operations, https://www.make.com/en/pricing
