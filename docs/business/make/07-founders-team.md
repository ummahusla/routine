## Founding Overview

**Company origin:** Integromat was conceived as an internal project and officially launched as a product in 2016, though the founding team began work together as early as 2012–2013 in Prague, Czech Republic. The company operated without external investment or debt from the start, remained fully self-funded and profitable, and was acquired by Celonis on October 14, 2020 for over $100 million (CZK 2.5 billion+). On February 22, 2022 it was rebranded as Make.

Source: Czech Startups Government Interview (https://czechstartups.gov.cz/en/novinky/interview-with-ondrej-gazda-and-patrik-simek-on-an-investment-that-resonates-with-the-startup-world-the-czech-company-integromat-was-acquired-by-the-german-firm-celonis-for-a-price-of-more-than-czk-2/); BusinessWire (https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20220222005231/en/Integromat-Evolves-to-Make-Expanding-Its-Vision-to-Empower-Creators-to-Innovate-Without-Limits); PRNewswire (https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/celonis-acquires-integromat-an-industry-leading-online-automation-platform-301152316.html)

## Company History: From Internal Tool to Acquisition

Source: Czech Startups Government Interview; CzechTheValley; Tracxn; Crunchbase (https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/integromat-2)

**Key timeline:**

- **2012–2013:** Initial project began in Prague, Czech Republic, as an internal tool within Cloud Service (a software company). The original purpose was to save the team time — the idea of turning it into a cloud integration platform came later.
- **2015:** Integromat began operations / the product started to take shape as a standalone platform.
- **2016:** Official launch of Integromat as a commercial product.
- **Feb–Apr 2017:** Integromat joined the US Market Access Center incubator program in San Francisco.
- **Nov–Dec 2017:** Integromat won the V4 Startup and Innovation Fastlane Tour and the Get in the Ring Budapest competition, winning an all-expenses-paid trip to Google Launchpad Tel Aviv.
- **2018:** Received the first acquisition offer from Celonis. Also received offers from other major SaaS companies. All were declined — the founders said companies were trying to acquire the technology and shut down the product.
- **2018–2020:** Turned down Celonis three times and other unnamed large SaaS acquirers. The founders cited loyalty to the product and users as the reason for declining.
- **October 14, 2020:** Celonis acquires Integromat for over $100 million (CZK 2.5 billion+). Confirmed by Crunchbase as approximately $107,644,175.
- **At time of acquisition:** 60 employees; 375,000+ users; 500+ out-of-the-box connectors; growing at 400%+ year-over-year; $10M+ revenue.
- **February 22, 2022:** Integromat officially rebrands to Make, launched as its own business unit within Celonis.

**Self-funded bootstrapped approach:**
> Integromat operated without external investments or debt since its founding in 2013 and was profitable for an extended period before the acquisition. No funding rounds were raised prior to the acquisition.

From the Czech Startups interview, on the founding philosophy:
> Integromat was a project whose primary purpose was to save them time — the idea of turning it into a cloud integration platform came only later, and end-user experience played a significant role in their decision-making processes.

## Celonis Parent Company: Co-CEOs and Their Relation to Make

Source: Celonis PRNewswire acquisition press release (https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/celonis-acquires-integromat-an-industry-leading-online-automation-platform-301152316.html); TechCrunch (https://techcrunch.com/2020/10/14/celonis-acquires-czech-startup-integromat-to-accelerate-move-to-process-automation/); Wikipedia (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celonis); TUM Community (https://www.community.tum.de/en/celonis/)

Celonis was founded in 2011 by three graduates of the Technical University of Munich (TUM):

**Alexander Rinke** — Co-CEO, Celonis
- Studied Mathematics at TUM
- Part of the Academy Consult student consulting group at TUM where the three met

**Bastian Nominacher** — Co-CEO, Celonis
- Studied Finance and Information Management at TUM; graduated 2011 with a Master's
- Met his co-founders through student consulting work at Bayerischer Rundfunk (Bavarian Broadcasting)

**Martin Klenk** — CTO, Celonis
- Graduated from TUM with a Bachelor's degree in Informatics

All three received the German Future Prize (Bundespreis für Technik und Innovation) from the German Federal President in 2019. They were named Entrepreneurs of the Year 2022 by Handelsblatt. Celonis was valued at $13 billion as of available reporting.

**Alexander Rinke quote on the Integromat acquisition** (October 14, 2020), from PRNewswire:

> "We knew that Celonis' first EMS acquisition had to be Integromat, the leader of the red-hot online automation space."

> "Integromat enables anybody in business to create easy, clean automations with the hundreds of systems that people all use in their jobs every day. We are blown away by the team, the product and the enthusiastic community with hundreds of thousands of Integromat users. Integromat will help our customers to turn the insights from Celonis into actions that unlock execution capacity."

On the deal price (from TechCrunch):
> "It was a three-digit amount of over $100 million. We can't disclose the exact price, but we're just giving this range."

**Connection to Make:** Celonis is the parent company of Make. Following the acquisition, Fabian Veit — who had been COO at Celonis and instrumental in the Integromat acquisition deal — was appointed CEO of Make when it launched as its own business unit in April 2022. Make operates as an independent entity within the Celonis portfolio.
