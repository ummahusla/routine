# Make.com Marketing Strategy

> Raw research dossier. All quotes are verbatim from primary sources. Every fact includes a source URL.

## Overview: Make's Growth Model

Make.com (formerly Integromat) operates a marketing strategy built on five primary pillars: organic SEO through comparison and integration content, a product-led growth (PLG) free tier, an annual in-person conference (Waves), an extensive partner and affiliate ecosystem, and a free Academy that converts users into certified advocates. Unlike Zapier's programmatic SEO machine, Make's organic strategy leans more heavily on direct competitor comparison pages, migration guides targeting Zapier/n8n defectors, and an education funnel (Make Academy) that doubles as a retention mechanism.

From the Make.com 2024 Automation Wrap-Up (https://www.make.com/en/blog/2024-automation-wrap-up):

> "More than a million new users discovered Make in 2024, growing the global community of Makers by 68% to 3.1 million."

> "A staggering 5.6 billion scenarios have been run—more than a billion more than in 2023, reflecting a 26% growth in usage."

From Latka (https://getlatka.com/companies/make.com):

> "Make hit $52.6M revenue with a 478 person team in 2025."

## SEO Strategy — Comparison Pages Targeting Competitors

### Official "Make vs Zapier" Page

Make published a direct competitor comparison on their blog targeting "make vs zapier" and "switch to make" search intent.

From the Make blog (https://www.make.com/en/blog/make-vs-zapier):

> Page title: "Make vs Zapier: How Are We Different?"

Key verbatim content from this page (via search result summaries):

> "Make generally offers roughly double the number of preset actions per app, meaning you can automate more within each integration without custom workarounds."

> "Make AI Agents are available on all paid plans and run natively inside the Scenario Builder."

> "To migrate from Zapier to Make, you start by familiarizing yourself with Make's terminology (Zaps become scenarios, steps become modules, and paths become routes), then recreate your Zaps as new scenarios in Make's Scenario Builder, with Make's 7,900+ scenario templates covering many common use cases."

> "Make provides professional support on all paid plans, with 24/7 priority customer support for Enterprise customers and additional resources including Make Community forum with 45,000+ registered members and Make Academy with 38 interactive courses."

Pricing positioning:
> "Make's most affordable paid plan is $9/month for 10,000 credits, while Zapier's paid plans start from $19.99/month for 750 tasks, meaning the price per action is significantly higher in Zapier than in Make."

### Official "Make vs n8n" Comparison Page

From the Make compare page (https://www.make.com/en/compare/make-vs-n8n):

> Page title: "Make vs N8N in 2026 | Compare features & pricing | Make"

> "While n8n offers deep developer customization, it requires significantly higher technical overhead and maintenance compared to Make's intuitive, enterprise-grade speed."

> "Make provides a visual-first platform for rapid AI orchestration across all skill levels."

> "Make offers flexible, credit-based plans that scale with workflow complexity and delivered value. Plans include: Free; Core; Pro; Teams and Enterprise."

> "Make provides a mature, comprehensive support system that scales with subscription level. The platform includes professional support, Make Academy training, and Make Community resources at no extra cost on all paid plans."

> "n8n offers official support only to users on the most expensive plans and is handled via email."

> "Cloud-based Make offers better reliability, tighter security, and lower total cost of ownership for most use cases."

> "The Make platform's drag-and-drop interface allows users to build automated workflows within the visual Scenario Builder with immediate visual feedback."

### No Official "Make vs Power Automate" Comparison Page

Based on research, Make does NOT appear to maintain an official dedicated `/compare/make-vs-power-automate` page as of May 2026. The comparison content targeting Microsoft Power Automate search terms is primarily produced by third-party publications (Collab365, ApiX-Drive, SaveMyLeads, ConsultEvo, Capterra, TrustRadius), not by Make's own blog or compare subdirectory.

### Integration Pages as SEO Asset

From Make's integrations hub (https://www.make.com/en/integrations):

> "3,000+ Integration Apps | Connect & Automate Software | Make"

The platform officially lists 3,000+ integrations. As of the 2024 wrap-up:

> "More than 500 new apps were added [in 2024], taking the total apps available to a record 2,100+"

Note: The 3,000+ figure refers to all apps available on the platform, while 2,100+ was the count specifically at end-of-2024. Third-party sources variously cite 2,000–3,000+ depending on the date of the article.

## Free Plan as Growth Lever (PLG Mechanism)

### Free Plan Features

From multiple sources including relay.app and thedigitalprojectmanager.com:

Make's Free plan includes:
- 1,000 operations per month
- 2 active scenarios
- Access to 3,000+ apps
- No-code visual workflow builder (Scenario Builder)
- Routers and filters
- Customer support
- 15-minute minimum interval between scheduled scenario runs

### Limitations That Drive Upgrades

From the Make Community (https://community.make.com/t/max-number-of-scenarios-operations-in-free-plan/16537):

> "Max number of scenarios/operations in free plan"

> "The free plan is not enough for real workflows and only works for testing and learning, as most real workflows with multiple steps exceed the free limits quickly."

> "Every change or poll counts for an operation, so it gets exhausted quickly."

### Upgrade Path

From Make's pricing tiers (as of 2026):

- **Free**: 1,000 operations/month, 2 active scenarios
- **Core**: $10.59/month for 10,000 credits, unlimited active scenarios
- **Pro**: $18.82/month, adds priority execution and full-text search
- **Teams**: $34.12/month, collaboration features, scenario templates
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing, overage protection, 24/7 support

Note: The free plan is positioned as a "try before you buy" mechanism. Make's pricing is operations-based rather than task-based, which allows Make to market a significantly lower price-per-action vs. Zapier.
