## Chronological Timeline

### 2005–2009: Jan's Education

- Jan Oberhauser studies Audiovisual Media at Hochschule der Medien (Stuttgart Media University)
- Source: Taskade history article (https://www.taskade.com/blog/n8n-workflow-automation-history)

### Post-2009: VFX Career

- Works at Academy Award-winning VFX studios
- Films: *Maleficent* (Disney), *Happy Feet Two*
- Studios: Digital Domain, Pixomondo, Neon
- Role evolution: effects artist → pipeline TD
- Source: Taskade article, Slush profile (https://slush.org/program/jan-oberhauser)

### While in College: showreel.tv

- Jan starts first company, showreel.tv, while still in college
- Source: Search results from Crunchbase

### Pre-2018: link.fish

- Jan co-founds link.fish
- This becomes his primary job while building n8n on the side
- Source: Multiple search results

### 2018: n8n Development Begins (Side Project)

- Jan starts building n8n as a side project while maintaining other startup involvement
- Works on it for approximately 1.5 years before public launch
- Source: Accel podcast (https://www.accel.com/podcast-episodes/bonus-n8ns-jan-oberhauser-on-building-the-excel-of-ai), Taskade article

### June 23, 2019: First GitHub Commit / Soft Launch

- Jan publishes the first version of n8n on GitHub
- Shares via alternativeto.net and Quora (not major tech channels yet)
- Source: n8n second anniversary blog (https://blog.n8n.io/celebrating-n8n-second-anniversary/)

> "June 23, 2019: First n8n version published" — Solo Founders article

### October 2019: Product Hunt and Hacker News Launch

- Proper public launch on Product Hunt and Hacker News
- n8n positioned as "open-source" alternative to Zapier
- Immediate backlash from Hacker News users about license (Apache 2.0 + Commons Clause)
- GitHub issue "It's gaslighting to call it open source" accumulates 330+ comments
- Hacker News changes post title from "n8n.io – Open source Zapier alternative" to "n8n.io – Workflow automation alternative to Zapier"
- Source: Jan's fair-code blog post (https://blog.n8n.io/fair-code-for-sustainable-open-source-alternatives/), Taskade article

### November 2019: 10,000 GitHub Stars

- Repository reaches 10,000 stars within 5 months of first GitHub publication
- Source: n8n second anniversary blog

> "The n8n repository gathered 5,000 stars only five months after publishing and 10,000 stars by November 2019."

- Launch faircode.io website
- **n8n becomes the first official fair-code project**
- Source: Jan's fair-code blog post (https://blog.n8n.io/fair-code-for-sustainable-open-source-alternatives/)

> "They met in San Francisco in early 2020 and formulated the definition together, launching a website at faircode.io."

## The "nodemation" Name — Origin Details

From multiple sources:

> While looking for a good name for the project with a free domain, Jan quickly realized that all the good ones he could think of were already taken. So, in the end, he chose "nodemation." 'node-' in the sense that it uses a Node-View and that it uses Node.js and '-mation' for 'automation' which is what the project is supposed to help with.

The abbreviation "n8n" follows the same convention as:
- i18n (internationalization — 18 letters between 'i' and 'n')
- k8s (Kubernetes — 8 letters between 'k' and 's')

n8n = n + 8 letters + n = "nodemation"

Pronunciation: "n eight n"

Always written in lowercase: "n8n"

Source: n8n press page (https://n8n.io/press/) and multiple articles
