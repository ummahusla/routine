### Career Before n8n

Source: Taskade article, Slush profile (https://slush.org/program/jan-oberhauser), Accel podcast, Turner Classic Movies database

**VFX Career (post-graduation):**
> Worked at Academy Award-winning VFX studios on major productions
> Films: Disney's *Maleficent* and *Happy Feet Two*
> Studios: Digital Domain, Pixomondo, and Neon

From Slush HQ (Twitter/X):
> "Before launching n8n, Jan worked as a VFX engineer at Academy Award–winning [studios]"

From the Accel podcast:
> Jan "did effects from movies originally" and later became a "pipeline entity" focused on making "the life of the artist more efficient, easier, which obviously involved a lot of automation."

**Role evolution in VFX:**
> Started as effects artist → became "pipeline TD" (Technical Director) → automated workflows for other artists

**Key insight from VFX work:**
> "Those people — very smart, very well paid, and quite technical — they were always reliant on me or other people like myself to actually do the things they wanted done. They could have had so much more impact if they would have been empowered. And they weren't."

**Prior companies:**
- showreel.tv — started while still in college
- link.fish — co-founded before n8n (Oberhauser was working here when building n8n as a side project)

From Crunchbase:
> Listed as "Founder/CEO @ n8n" with prior role at link.fish

### Founding n8n

**From the Accel podcast (https://www.accel.com/podcast-episodes/bonus-n8ns-jan-oberhauser-on-building-the-excel-of-ai):**

> "I spent probably 90% of my time re-implementing things that have been implemented before. The most basic example is something like Get us down GitHub, send a message to Slack."

> "Why do I spend most of my time on reimplementing things that have been done and they're not very joyful to do?"

Timeline:
- 2018: First implementation began (side project)
- 1.5 years building while working other jobs to support family
- June 23, 2019: First GitHub publication
- October 2019: Product Hunt and Hacker News launch

**Declined Y Combinator:**
From TechCrunch (https://techcrunch.com/2020/03/13/n8n-a-fair-code-workflow-automation-platform-raises-seed-from-sequoia-as-vc-firm-steps-up-in-europe/):
> Oberhauser **declined Y Combinator to remain in Berlin with family.**

### Leadership Style and Philosophy

**From the Accel podcast:**

On hiring:
> Prefers "humble excellence" and "people who are quietly brilliant"
> Key hiring traits: "People are humble," "Always there to help others," care deeply about the work, right motivation (excitement, not wealth-seeking)
> "He was doing a good job, but everybody had ended and cared very deeply about what we were doing and he didn't have the same excitement." (on one early termination)

On company culture:
> "High-velocity, high-trust, low-ego, and all-in" (from careers page)
> Employee NPS: "95 to a hundred depending on the half year"

On the business model:
> "Do a few things right versus doing everything kind of half-baked. I think it's generally a good recipe."
> "I'm not building n8n for free because I'm a good person. I genuinely need to build a sustainable company"

On AI:
> "I honestly was a bit scared" when AI emerged, but realized "this thing that fits perfectly together."

On the AI vision:
> "What was Excel 15 years ago for spreadsheet is, and it should be become the same for ai, so really empowering everybody to use AI to the fullest and get real value out of it."

On building the company efficiently:
> "First 1 billion Euro R company with less than 500 employees"

On European IPO (from Taskade article):
> "I definitely want a European listing"

**From the Series C announcement:**
> "The AI race isn't only about smarter models - it's about who can actually put that intelligence to work reliably, inside actual businesses."

**From the Series B announcement:**
> "Automation shouldn't be a black box – companies need transparency, customisation and cost efficiency"

**From the fair-code blog post:**
> "I'm not building n8n for free because I'm a good person. I genuinely need to build a sustainable company"

## Team Demographics and Culture

Source: https://n8n.io/careers/ (fetched May 2026)

> "30+ different nationalities working from 18 different countries"

**Operating principles / core values:**
1. **Empower others:** "Trust by default, ownership, autonomy, and making AI orchestration accessible"
2. **Integrity:** "Transparency, direct communication, honesty, and accountability"
3. **Speed and efficiency:** "Thoughtful and practical movement, shipping over perfection"
4. **Inspire and think big:** "Builder mindset focused on testing, learning, and transformative ambition"

**Ideal candidate profile:**
> "Curious and hands-on builders"
> "Technical empathy across all roles"
> "Genuine passion for AI and automation"
> "'Impatient to make things real' with bias for shipping fast"
> "Community-minded approach"

**Culture description:**
> "High-velocity, high-trust, low-ego, and all-in" where "titles matter less than ideas" and "everyone has a direct line to leadership."

**Employee benefits:**
- Remote-first, global talent model
- Healthy vacation allowance
- Learning budget for courses/conferences
- Flexible care support
- Fair pay with regular benchmarking
- Regular company offsites

**Departments:**
> Sales, Marketing, Engineering, Operations, Product & Design, and People teams

**Company offsites:**
From the 2024 year-in-review (https://blog.n8n.io/2024-in-review/):
> "Held two in-person company gatherings (Berlin and Tuscany)"

**Transparency practices:**
From Series A blog post (https://blog.n8n.io/series-a-announcement/):
> $100 monthly stipend per employee for open source donations
> Quarterly hackathons for team building
